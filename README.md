# radio-operator-field-guide-level-1
Give this zine to a brand-new technician and say: “This is how you can use your radio today.”

A no-BS, field-ready guide to getting on the air fast.

This started as a small printed zine for Ham Radio Village at DEF CON 33.  
It’s still a small printed zine.

The goal is simple:
> Cut through the noise, provide just enough real info to:
- make a contact  
- build a basic antenna  
- find a fox  
- understand what they’re doing  

…without wading through a textbook.

We were all new once.  
What did you wish you knew on day one?

Also note: if you just passed your exam at the con and don’t have your license yet — what can you still do in the meantime?

---

## What this is NOT

- Not a replacement for the ARRL Handbook or any serious reference  
  (this fits on a single sheet — manage expectations)

- Not legal advice  
  (seriously — verify your local laws and band plan)

- Not a universal frequency guide  
  (this version is US-leaning; international use will vary)

- Not an exam prep tool or by any means all-encompasing

---

## Philosophy

- Practical > theoretical  
- Short > complete  
- Actionable > academic  

If it doesn’t help someone:
- get on the air  
- build good habits  
- find a fox  
- troubleshoot  
- build something  

…it probably doesn’t belong.

## What’s inside (Level 1) page listing

1. Cover (includes version number, QR code to source, somewhere to write your callsign)
